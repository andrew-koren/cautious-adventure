Q2

This is a sort of change in volume, with $f$ instead of $p$ and $\delta l$ instead of $\delta V$ in 1D.

$$ \tau d \sigma = dU + p dV \\ \Downarrow \\ \tau d \sigma = dU - f dl $$

We might have to re-derive the free energy $F$, but we'll get there later.

(a) Our system is quantized/discrete, with N links each left or right facing, and a total of $2^N$ possible states. Aka binomial! $s$ is the spin (flip) excess $s =(s_\leftarrow - s_\rightarrow)/2$

For N=3 the possible arrangements are: $$ \leftarrow \leftarrow \leftarrow l=3\rho \quad \rightarrow \rightarrow \leftarrow l=\rho\\ \leftarrow \leftarrow \rightarrow l=\rho \quad \rightarrow \leftarrow \rightarrow l=\rho\\ \leftarrow \rightarrow \leftarrow l=\rho \quad \leftarrow \rightarrow \rightarrow l=\rho\\ \rightarrow \leftarrow \leftarrow l=\rho \quad \rightarrow \rightarrow \rightarrow l=3\rho\\ $$

Okay, quick sanity check works out. Since this is binomial we can use our spin-excess multiplicity formula.

The number of states with flip excess $s$ is

$$ g(N,s) = \frac{N!}{(N/2 + s)!(N/2-s)!} $$

Since $l = 2|s|\rho = 2|-s|\rho$, negative and positive excess have the same length

$$ g(l) = g(N,|s|) = g(N,-s)+ g(N,s) = 2g(N,s) $$

(b) $\sigma(l) = \ln g(l)$, also note $s = \frac{l}{2 \rho}$

Using our approximation $g(N,s) \approx 2^N e^{-2s^2/N}$, we have

$$ g(l) = 2 g(N,s) \approx 2 g(N,0) e^{-2s^2/N} \\ \ln g(l) \approx \ln(2 g(N,0) e^{-2s^2/N}) = \ln(2 g(N,0)) + \ln(e^{-2s^2/N}) \\ = \ln(2 g(N,0)) -2s^2/N \\ = \ln(2 g(N,0)) -l^2/2 N\rho^2 $$

(c) $f = -\tau \frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial l}_U$

$$ \frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial l} \left(- \frac{l^2}{2 N \rho^2} \right) = -\frac{l}{N \rho^2} \\ f = -\tau \left(-\frac{l}{N \rho^2} \right) = \frac{l\tau}{N \rho^2} $$
