Q1

(a) We will apply cross-differentiation to get these results.

From the textbook:

$$ \frac{\partial G}{\partial N}_{\tau,p} = \mu \\ \frac{\partial G}{\partial \tau}_{N,p} = - \sigma \\ \frac{\partial G}{\partial p}_{N,\tau} = V \\ $$

We find that

$$ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial p} = \frac{\partial^2 G}{\partial p \partial N} & \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \tau} = \frac{\partial^2 G}{\partial \tau \partial N} \\ \frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial p} = -\frac{\partial^2 G}{\partial p \partial \tau} & \frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial N} = -\frac{\partial^2 G}{\partial \tau \partial N} \\ \frac{\partial V}{\partial N} = \frac{\partial^2 G}{\partial p \partial N} & \frac{\partial V}{\partial \tau} = \frac{\partial^2 G}{\partial p \partial \tau} \end{matrix} $$

Combining RHS like terms yields the maxwell relations.
