(a) A photon gas has energy

$$ U = \sum_j s_j \hbar \omega_j $$

Since entropy is held constant, the photons in $s_j$ will be constant as volume changes $ds/dV = 0$.

$$ p = - \frac{\partial}{\partial V} \sum_j s_j \hbar \omega_j = -\sum_j s_j \hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial V} \omega_j $$

(b) $\omega$ is related to the wave vector $k$, which must be a multiple of $n$ as discussed in the book. Using $\omega_j = c n_j \frac{\pi}{L}$ and $V = L^3$, we have

$$ \frac{d}{dV} \omega_j = c n_j \pi \frac{d}{dV} \frac{1}{L} \\ 1/L = 1/\sqrt[3]{V} \frac{d}{dV} \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{-3V^{4/3}} = \frac{1}{-3LV} \\ \frac{d}{dV} \omega_j = c n_j \pi\left( \frac{1}{L} \frac{-1}{3V} \right)= -\frac{\omega_j}{3V} $$

(c) Just plug in

$$ p = - \sum_j \frac{s_j \hbar \omega_j}{-3V} = \frac{U}{3V} $$
