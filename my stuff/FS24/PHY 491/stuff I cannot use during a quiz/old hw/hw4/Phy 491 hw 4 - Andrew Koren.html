Phy 491 - Hw 4¶

Oxygen - the homework assignment

O: $1s^22s^22p^4$

Spectroscopic notation: $\ ^{2s+1}L_{J} $. Not to be confused with Molecular term symbols $\ ^{2S+1} \Lambda_{g,u}$

$\sum s=1/2 + 1/2 = 1 \rightarrow 2s+1=3$
$\sum l=1 \rightarrow S$ - hund's rule #2
More than half full: $J = L + S = 2$

Result: $\ ^{3}S_{2}$

Bonding: 10
Antibonding: 8
Stability: $B = \frac{10-8}{2}=1$

Since $B>0$, the molecule is stable

Note: see Laporte's rules for g/u

Unexcited:
$S = 2*1/2 = 1 \rightarrow 2S+1 = 3$
$L=0 \rightarrow \Epsilon$. $g*g=g$

$$ ^3\Sigma_g $$

Excited (1): $S = 0$, $L=0$

$$ ^1 \Sigma_g $$

Excited (2): $S = 0, L=2$

$$ ^1\Delta_g $$

Ground state: $|J|=1$ yields weakly paramagnetic.

Excited states (1): Diamagnetic as spin momentum cancels

Excited states (2): Strongly paramagnetic. While spin still sums to zero, the combined electrons in a single orbital yields $L=2\rightarrow J=2$
