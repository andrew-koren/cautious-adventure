5.1.2. 3D (BCC) -> $\frac{N}{V} = \frac{2}{a^3}$

Using our Debye frequency as $$ \omega_D^3 = 6\pi^2 \frac{N}{V} v^3 = \frac{12\pi^2 v_s^3}{a^3}\\ \omega_D = \sqrt[3]{\frac{12\pi^2 v_s^3}{a^3}} = \frac{v_s}{a}\sqrt[3]{12\pi^2 } $$ we have $$ \omega_D = \frac{6e5}{3.5e-8}\sqrt[3]{12\pi^2} = 8.42\times 10^{13} \text{Hz} \\ =84.2 \text{Thz} \\ $$ For temperature, we'll use $k_B T_D = \hbar \omega $ $$ T_D = \frac{\hbar \omega}{k_B} = 1425 K $$

This is quite far off from the experimental result listed in Kittel (335K). Using the formula listed for the 1D chain we get 411K, which is a bit closer.

5.2 We need to find $C=\frac{dE}{dT}$, so we need to find $\langle E \rangle(T)$

$$ \langle E \rangle = \int_0^\infty d \omega g(\omega) \hbar \omega n_B(\beta \hbar \omega) $$ The only tough part is $g(\omega)$. In class we used $g(\omega) = N \frac{9\omega^2}{\omega_D^3}$, but that was in a 3D case.

Note that $N(\omega) = \int_0^{\omega_D} g(\omega) d\omega$, which comes out to 3N using the above formula for $g(\omega)$. The 3*N comes from the 3 polarization of phonons

In 1D we should have our $g(\omega)$ be independent of frequencey and only have N modes. I came up with

$$ g(\omega) = \frac{N}{\omega_D} $$

Where $\omega_D = v\frac{\pi}{a}$ (which I stole from your formula for $T_D$)

Now we can do the integral!

$$ x = \beta \hbar \omega \\ dx = \beta \hbar d\omega \rightarrow d\omega = \frac{dx}{\beta \hbar}\\ \langle E \rangle = \int_0^\infty d\omega \frac{N}{\omega_D} \hbar \omega \frac{1}{e^{\beta\hbar \omega}-1} \\ = \int_0^\infty \frac{dx}{\beta \hbar} \frac{N}{\omega_D} \frac{x}{\beta} \frac{1}{e^{x}-1} \\ = \frac{1}{\beta^2 \hbar}\frac{N}{\omega_D} \int_0^\infty dx \frac{x}{e^{x}-1} = \frac{T^2}{ \hbar}\frac{N}{\omega_D} \frac{\pi^2}{6} $$

Which gives us our heat capacity

$$ C = \frac{T}{ \hbar}\frac{N}{\omega_D} \frac{\pi^2}{3} \\ = \frac{\pi^2}{3} \frac{TNa}{v \pi} = \frac{\pi}{3} \frac{Na}{v}T $$

This has $N$ dependence, but I'm sure that isn't an issue
