import numpy as np
from numpy import cos, pi

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

e0 = 1
a = 1
params = [e0, a]

x = np.linspace(-pi/a, pi/a, 1000)
y = np.linspace(-pi/a, pi/a, 1000)

kx, ky = np.meshgrid(x, y)

def tight_binding(kx, ky, params):
    e0, a = params
    return e0*(2-cos(a*kx)-cos(a*ky))

energy = tight_binding(kx, ky, params)

ax = plt.figure().add_subplot(111, projection='3d')
ax.plot_surface(kx, ky, energy, cmap='viridis')
ax.view_init(35, 20)
plt.title('Energy landscape, arbitrary units')
plt.tight_layout()

4.1.1

$\Gamma$: $k_x,k_y=0$ $$E(0,0) = 0$$

$X$: $k_x,k_y=0,\frac{\pi}{a}$ or $\frac{\pi}{a},0$ $$ E\left(\frac{\pi}{a},0\right)=E\left(0,\frac{\pi}{a}\right)=\epsilon_0 $$

$W$: $k_x, K_y = \frac{\pi}{a}$ $$ E\left(\frac{\pi}{a},\frac{\pi}{a}\right) =2\epsilon_0 $$

4.1.5 The energy is $2\epsilon_0$. See the plot below. This is definitely a metal. Since two electrons are needed to fill each site, only half of the sites are filled. Any voltage can push electrons to fill sites above the usual Fermi energy, which can cause current flow. Thanks to the diamond shape that we get from $E=2\epsilon_0$, half the area is below the line and half is above.

4.1.6 This is an insulator. Since there is one orbital for each atom, and two electrons for each atom, all orbitals have two electrons and are filled due to the Pauli exlusion princliple. Electrons cannot absorb energy, since they are 'quantized' and cannot move to another orbital without entirely escaping. No current can flow since electrons cannot move to new locations.
