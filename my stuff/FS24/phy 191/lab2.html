Theory

Small standard deviation

a. Considering that the system we're dealing with is a singular clock, each $\delta T_i$ should be the same standard deviation $\sigma$ in clock measurement.

b. $$\frac{\partial \bar T}{\partial T_1} = \frac{d}{dT_1}\frac{T_1}{N} = \frac{1}{N}$$

c. This is simple. Since $\bar T$ is just the sum of each term divided by $N$, each term has the same shape/operation to compute the partial derivative $$\frac{\partial \bar T}{\partial T_i} = \frac{d}{dT_i}\frac{T_i}{N} = \frac{1}{N} $$

d. Using the above formula $$ \delta \bar T = \sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{N} \sigma \right)^2 + \left(\frac{1}{N} \sigma \right)^2 + \dots + \left(\frac{1}{N} \sigma \right)^2} \\ = \sqrt{\frac{\sigma^2}{N^2} + \frac{\sigma^2}{N^2} + \dots + \frac{\sigma^2}{N^2}} \\ = \sqrt{N\frac{\sigma^2}{N^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{\sigma^2}{N}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{N}} $$

a. Since $N=2$: $$\bar x = \frac{x_1+x_2}{2}$$

b. Equation 5: $$ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{1} \sum_{i=1}{2}(x_i-\bar x)^2 }\\ = \sqrt{(x_1-\bar x)^2 + (x_2-\bar x)^2}$$

c. $$ \sigma = \sqrt{\left(x_1-\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}\right)^2 + \left(x_2-\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}\right)^2} \\ = \sqrt{\left(\frac{x_1-x_2}{2}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{x_2-x_1}{2}\right)^2} \\ = \sqrt{2\left(\frac{x_1-x_2}{2}\right)^2 } = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \left|x_1-x_2\right| $$

d.

$$ \delta \bar x = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_1 - x_2 \right| $$

e.

The error bar's ends coincide with the values $x_1$ and $x_2$

Introduction

  1. Drawing Conclusions

1 Count rate estimate

We should be able to simply divide our mean time by $10$ to get our sec/count.

$$\frac{9.69}{10} = 0.969 \text{ sec/count} $$

Standard error in a mean depends on the standard deviation. If we divide each measurement by 10, the new standard deviation is 0.0243

In[15]:
measured_1_count = measured_times / 10
measured_1_count.std()
Out[15]:

It makes sense that this is 1/10 the standard error previously found. It also yields a reduced standard error of 0.004 instead of 0.04

measured_1_count.std() / np.sqrt(len(measured_1_count))

Our resulting mean is $0.969 \pm 0.004$. The relative error is the same, which makes sense since our measurements are the same

2 t-test

$$ t = \frac{|R_{ref}-\bar R}{\delta \bar R} = \frac{1-0.969}{0.004} \\= \frac{0.031}{0.004} = 7.75 $$

3 Our results indicate that the clock has a systematic error, and a large one. It is not likely that one count is one second
